ASL Fingerspelling Practice

A random word is displayed one letter at a time using ASL handshapes. Use the speed slider to control the pace, step through manually with the arrow buttons, or type your own word to practice spelling it.

—
 
Speed: Medium

Each handshape represents one letter of the English alphabet. Letters J and Z involve motion — the image shows the starting hand position. Use ◀ ▶ to step through letters manually, or press Play to run automatically at your chosen speed.

Fingerspelling is used in ASL primarily for proper nouns — names of people, places, and brands — and for English words that have no established ASL sign. Visit Lifeprint.com for full ASL lessons, a sign language dictionary, and teaching resources.
